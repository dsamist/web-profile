Brief History

A System Administrator turned DevOps Engineer with a passion for infrastructure and process automation

I started my professional journey with Wema Bank as a system administrator and later transition to working as a cloud administrator and a DevOps engineer. Since then I have had the opportunity to work as a DevOps engineer at Irembo and SLS Energy, and as a Teaching Assistant (TA) for the courses "Cloud Infrastructure and Computing" and "Leading and Managing Technology and Innovation" at Carnegie Mellon University. Who am I and What I do

Wema Bank Plc

System Administrator

As an integral part of the Wema Bank Infrastructure team, I worked as a System Administrator for more than 3 years helping to build and setup systems and servers in data centers and implementing virtualization. In my time as a system administrator, I was responsible for ensuring constant up-time of on-premises server infrastructures and ensured proper resynchronization and replication of virtual machines for backup and disaster recovery purpose. I also conducted routine system updates, patches, and security audits to ensure a secure and up-to-date infrastructure for a multi-platform environment consisting of both windows and Linux operating systems.

Irembo

DevOps Engineer

As a DevOps Engineer, I started with cultivating the DevOps culture when I joined Irembo. I began working on projects that shifted my knowledge from the traditional monolith to agile implementation and assisting to automate processes from end-to-end. Right from Building the code (CI) to deploying it to different enviornments (CD). Then, I also learnt the creation of infrastructure with the help of code (IAC).

SLS Energy

DevOps Engineer

With the years spent in working as a system administrator and DevOps in my previous roles, I have gained knowlege of cloud service providers like Microsoft Azure and AWS, and have a thorough understanding of CI and CD processes. With these knowledge, I spearheaded the migration of SLS monitoring platform infrastructure to Amazon Elastic Kubernetes Service (AWS EKS), optimizing resource utilization, application availability and performance.
